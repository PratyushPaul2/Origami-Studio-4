ORIGAMI DESIGNS

Origami is an ancient Japanese art of folding paper. The word comes from two Japanese words meaning fold paper. Here are some facts about Origami:-


1. In Japan, origami has inspired a lot of art, fashion, food, and even architecture. If you visit Japan you will find many buildings based on origami style.


2. There are more than a dozen National Origami Associations throughout the world.


3. Akira Yoshizawa is considered the Grand Master of modern Origami. He created over 50,000 origami models, invented wet-folding, and developed a method of diagramming origami instructions.


4. The Guinness Book of World Records has dozens of entries for origami from most folds, to smallest and biggest and more!


5. The fastest time to make 100 origami cranes is 40 minutes 35 seconds, achieved by Yoneyama Yuichi in Nagoya, Japan. Thatâ€™s an average of 24.35 seconds for each crane!

6. Initially, the art of paper folding was restricted only to ceremonial occasions because paper was scarce and expensive.
